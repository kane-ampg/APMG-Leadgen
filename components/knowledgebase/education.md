# APMG Services — Education & Schools Sector Knowledge Base

Australian Property Maintenance Group (APMG Services) is a Victoria-based, multi-trade property maintenance provider that specialises in the education and childcare sectors — kindergartens, early learning centres, schools, colleges and higher-education campuses. APMG offers a single-partner solution across painting, carpentry, flooring, electrical, plumbing, gardening/grounds, handyman and property make-safe work, with a focus on keeping learning environments safe, clean, compliant and operational. Its positioning is to "simplify property maintenance by delivering trusted, high-quality trade solutions through one reliable partner."

### Grounds maintenance (per sample Scope of Works)
Scheduled/planned grounds maintenance is delivered under documented fixed-scope visits and can include: mowing and edging; car park sweeping/blowing and drain clearing; playground, verandah, pergola and sand-pit cleaning; tree, shrub and hedge trimming/pruning; garden bed weeding and maintenance; and fascia gutter/downpipe inspection and cleaning. (Note: certain tasks are explicitly excluded in the sample — e.g. pruning/gutter cleaning above 3 metres, and weekend attendance for the fixed scope.)

The sample Scope of Works also sets out operational parameters. These are illustrative sample-document details, not firm universal commitments:
- A **2-hour time allocation** commences for the fixed-scope tasks per visit.
- **4 hours per site must be fulfilled**, delivered across **2 x 2-hour visits**.
- If all fixed-scope tasks are completed within a visit, the technician is to **assist the Centre Director (CD) with small handyman works**.
- **Materials/ancillary allocation:** ancillary items used in providing the service (e.g. fuel, rags, bags, waste removal) are included in the fixed price; any additional materials are itemised on the monthly invoice (with receipts retained for presentation if requested).

### Sustainability-linked service practices
- Low-VOC / eco-friendly and certified sustainable paints and materials where practical
- Responsible construction/maintenance waste separation, disposal and recycling to reduce landfill
- Electrical work prioritising LED upgrades, smart systems and energy-efficient fittings
- Water-efficient fixtures — dual-flush systems, flow restrictors, sustainable irrigation
- Make-safe / repair / maintenance-first approach to extend asset life and avoid premature replacement
- Locally based teams and subcontractors to reduce travel and vehicle emissions

## Sector-Specific Needs / Pain Points APMG Addresses
- **Minimising disruption to learning:** flexible scheduling including evenings, weekends and term breaks so classroom operations stay uninterrupted.
- **Child safety and safeguarding:** work delivered around sensitive schedules and high-risk, child-centric environments; staff vetted and trained for these spaces.
- **Compliance:** adherence to OH&S and child-safe standards, plus safety and regulatory requirements for high-traffic and emergency areas.
- **Keeping facilities operational and presentable:** helping learning environments "stay operational, welcoming and up to standard."
- **Coordination with school/centre leadership:** working closely with centre managers and school leaders to align scope with schedules, priorities and safety.
- **Single point of accountability:** consolidating many trades under "one reliable partner" so facilities don't juggle multiple contractors.
- **Documented, accountable delivery:** clear scopes, project management, before/after photo records, job sheets with hazard checks, and full handover documentation.

- **Education-and-childcare specialisation:** deep familiarity with kindergartens, early learning centres, schools, colleges and campuses, and their specific safety/compliance needs.

- **Embedded sustainability:** eco-friendly materials, waste recycling, energy- and water-efficient practices, and local sourcing.
