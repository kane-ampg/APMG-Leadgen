# APMG Services — Aged Care & Health Sector Knowledge Base

APMG (Australian Property Maintenance Group / "APMG Services") is a multi-trade property-maintenance company that delivers reliable, high-quality trade solutions to the Aged Care and Health sector through one reliable partner. APMG covers scheduled maintenance, emergency repairs, and major upgrades across many trades (painting, carpentry/joinery, flooring, electrical, plumbing, grounds, handyman, and make-safe), with an explicit focus on safety, hygiene, regulatory compliance, and minimal disruption in occupied care environments. Work is delivered with "professionalism, compassion, and care at the core of every service," documented through a formal planning, project-management, and quality-assurance/handover process.

> Sector focus note: This KB is for the Aged Care & Health sector. APMG's mission statement also names industrial, commercial, and residential clients, and its values/portfolio reference hospitality — where APMG describes delivering "fast, discreet, and high quality outcomes that support guest experience and brand standards." The sample project-management documents reference childcare/school grounds work. Keep outreach anchored on aged care and health unless the recipient's own business clearly spans those other areas.

### Internal fit-outs & residential spaces
High-quality internal fit-out and reinstatement works for aged care and retirement living facilities, delivered with attention to durability, accessibility, and minimal disruption in resident-occupied spaces (safety upgrades through to modernisation projects):
- Wall-mounted noticeboard and signage installations
- Joinery repairs and replacements (bedrooms, nurse stations, dining areas)
- Non-slip flooring upgrades in wet areas and resident rooms
- Internal repainting and touch-ups in high-traffic corridors
- Tap, basin, and sink maintenance in kitchenettes and activity rooms
- Laundry and utility joinery repairs
- Reinstatement works following room vacates or upgrades
- Acoustic panel installations in communal areas and dining spaces
- Lighting upgrades for visibility and energy efficiency

### Safety & compliance works
Practical, discreet, regulation-aligned solutions for the risks of caring for older residents, implemented in close coordination with facility managers:
- Ramp and handrail installations for improved mobility and access
- Trip hazard rectification (uneven paving, walkways, flooring transitions)
- Anti-slip coatings for bathrooms, corridors, and entryways
- Emergency lighting and exit signage testing
- Secure fencing and gate maintenance (prevent unauthorised access)
- Dome mirrors in blind-spot areas to reduce collision risks
- CCTV installation and maintenance support structures
- Anti-graffiti coatings for street-facing or high-risk areas

### Community outdoor / grounds works
Maintaining safe, accessible, and peaceful outdoor environments to support resident wellbeing, delivered with minimal disruption:
- Garden path resurfacing and trip hazard rectification
- Shade sail installation and repairs for courtyards and sitting areas
- Secure fencing and gate maintenance for resident safety
- Outdoor seating, handrail, and shelter repairs
- Drinking fountain servicing and upgrades
- Synthetic turf installation with shock-absorbing layers
- Outdoor lighting upgrades for visibility and security
- Drainage and irrigation maintenance in communal garden spaces
- Quiet repainting or maintenance of outdoor relaxation zones

### Scheduled grounds-maintenance servicing (documented scope)
APMG runs formal, recurring grounds-maintenance visits under a fixed scope (evidenced by their Scope of Works sample), covering: mowing and edging, car park sweeping/blowing and drain clearing, playground/verandah/pergola/patio cleaning, tree trimming and pruning, garden bed upkeep and weeding, and fascia gutter/downpipe inspection and cleaning. Servicing is scheduled on weekdays within normal operating hours at an agreed day and time. The documented scope allocates 2 hours per visit across two visits, with a minimum of 4 hours per site; if all fixed-scope tasks are completed within the visits, the technician is instructed to assist the Centre Director (CD) with any small handyman works. (Note: some scoped tasks carry stated exclusions, e.g. pruning and gutter cleaning above 3 metres, and weekend attendance — see Guardrails.)

### Sustainability-linked service options
Sustainability is described as "embedded in everything we do." Relevant, sellable practices include:
- Low-impact materials and eco-friendly / low-VOC paints "where practical"
- Responsible waste separation and disposal via recycling partners to reduce landfill
- Electrical LED upgrades, smart systems, and energy-efficient fittings to cut long-term energy use
- Water-efficient fixtures: dual-flush systems, flow restrictors, and sustainable irrigation
- A make-safe / repair / maintenance-first approach that extends asset life and reduces premature replacement
- Locally based teams and subcontractors to reduce travel and vehicle emissions

## Sector-specific needs / pain points APMG addresses

- **Minimal disruption in occupied environments** — recurring promise across every service area; work is planned around resident routines and care delivery.
- **Safety, accessibility, and mobility** — ramps, handrails, anti-slip coatings, trip-hazard rectification, dome mirrors, secure fencing/gates.
- **Regulatory compliance** — work performed to "all regulations," with regulation-aligned safety solutions and formal documentation.
- **Hygiene and comfort** — clean, safe, comfortable spaces for residents, staff, and patients.
- **Resident dignity and wellbeing** — empathy, patience, and respect toward residents, patients, and staff; peaceful, calm environments.
- **Coordination with facility managers and care teams** — understanding routines, priorities, and resident needs before work begins.
- **Reliability and responsiveness** — dependable, quick to act, for time-critical and trust-critical care settings; scheduled maintenance plus emergency repairs.
- **Reinstatement / turnover works** — restoring rooms after vacates or upgrades.
- **Multi-trade demand under one roof** — reducing the overhead of managing many separate contractors.

## Guardrails for the email writer

**You MAY:**
- Reference any trade or specific service listed above (painting, carpentry/joinery, flooring, electrical, plumbing, grounds/gardening, handyman, make-safe) and the named aged-care internal, safety/compliance, and outdoor service lines.
- Emphasise APMG's core promises: one reliable multi-trade partner, aged-care/health specialisation, minimal disruption, safety and regulatory compliance, hygiene, resident dignity/wellbeing, and reliability/responsiveness.
- Cite the accreditations and memberships exactly as listed (Paintback, Dulux Accredited Painter, HIA member, MPA/Master Painters Australia, Working with Children Check, cm3, SAM4 SCHOOLS) — do not upgrade "member" to "certified/licensed" or invent scopes.
- Mention scheduled maintenance, emergency repairs, and major upgrades (all stated), and the four-stage approach and QA/handover process.
- Name real clients (Blue Cross, Wintringham, Brotherhood of St Laurence, TriCare, Oak Tree Retirement Villages, Barrina Gardens) as social proof — but do not describe specific projects, results, or endorsements, as none are given.
- Use the verbatim brand phrases above to keep tone consistent.
- Reference the Victoria (Chirnside Park) base and the contact details.

**You MAY NOT:**
- Invent statistics, years in business, project counts, team size, response times, warranties/guarantees, insurance, awards, or pricing — none appear in the source.
- Claim national coverage, 24/7 availability, or same-day service — the source states "emergency repairs" but gives no availability or coverage figures.
- Claim a service APMG does not list. If a recipient needs something outside the listed trades/services, do not assert APMG provides it.
- Use the wrong company name. APMG = **Australian Property Maintenance Group**. Do not write "Australian Painting Maintenance Group" (that appears only as a blurred, uncertain hoodie caption and is likely a misread).
- Overstate sustainability: it is "low-VOC paints where practical" and certified materials "where practical" — do not claim fully green/carbon-neutral operations or 100% recycled/eco materials.
- Ignore stated exclusions when describing grounds maintenance (e.g., pruning/gutter work above 3 metres and weekend attendance can be out of standard scope) — do not promise unlimited scope.
- Fabricate testimonials, case-study outcomes, or quotes from the named clients.
- Attribute the childcare/school grounds specifics (playgrounds, sand pits, Centre Director "CD" / small-handyman fill-in tasks) or the hospitality "guest experience and brand standards" language to aged-care work; those come from sample documents and value statements for other sectors.

**Always tailor to the recipient:** Identify the recipient's specific aged-care/health context (e.g., residential aged care, retirement village, health facility) and lead with the APMG services most relevant to that setting — resident-safety and accessibility works for care providers, grounds and outdoor-space upkeep for facilities with gardens/courtyards, internal fit-out/reinstatement for operators refreshing or turning over rooms. Match the pain point to APMG's listed capability; never bolt on a claim to fit a recipient's need.
